Proportion of purchase price financed by mortgage. Higher LTV = more leverage, higher risk to equity. Set 0% for cash purchase.
Expected duration of ownership before resale. The single most important input — transaction costs require 5–10 years to amortise.
Full repayment mortgage duration (standard repayment mode only). If holding period is shorter, outstanding principal is repaid at sale.
Transaction taxes, legal, surveyor and arrangement fees. UK Stamp Duty can reach 7%+ on higher-value properties.
Upfront capital not incurred when renting. Treated as unrecoverable at resale.
Agent fees, legal costs and transaction taxes on resale. Deducted from future resale proceeds.
Expected annual nominal appreciation. Short-run deviations from economic growth can persist 10+ years in supply-constrained markets.
Annual gross rent for an equivalent furnished property as % of current price. Basis for imputed rent (rent saved by owning).
Annual insurance as % of property value. Deducted from imputed rent because it is included in the landlord's rental charge.
Annual cost to maintain property value in real terms. Covered by landlord when renting. May include owner-only property taxes.
Expected annual growth in imputed rents (GRₙ). In a mature economy often set equal to inflation (real growth = 0). Can exceed inflation in supply-constrained urban markets.
Annual rate (rₑ). Fixed or assumed average over the holding period. Determines periodic payments and outstanding principal at sale.
Opportunity cost of capital (rₙ) — risk-free rate + risk/illiquidity premium for housing equity. Reflects forgone returns if the down payment were invested elsewhere. Higher LTV and shorter holding periods warrant a higher rate.
Expected annual CPI. Inflation strongly favours ownership — it erodes mortgage debt in real terms and raises nominal imputed rents. Deflation reverses this; many purchases may never break even under sustained deflation.
Interest-only mortgage held open indefinitely — principal never repaid. The pledged amount is invested as bank collateral; you keep the net investment gain above the original pledge when it is released.
15%
Amount locked in an investment account as bank guarantee. Additional cash outflow at t=0. Typically 15% in Swiss-style nantissement mortgages.
5%
Expected annual return on the pledged collateral portfolio. Net gain above the original pledged amount is returned to you at sale as an inflow.
15 yrs
How long the pledged collateral is locked as bank guarantee. Investment grows throughout this period (or the holding period if shorter).
Rate of tax on rent saved by owning. Zero in most countries (UK, US) but not all — Switzerland taxes a notional rental value.
Rate of tax relief reducing effective financing cost. Largely eliminated in UK and US. Benefits higher-rate taxpayers disproportionately.
CGT on nominal profit at resale. Zero for primary residence in many jurisdictions (UK, US main home exemption).
Tax on income if capital were invested outside housing. Where imputed rent is untaxed, ownership is advantaged relative to taxable investments.
